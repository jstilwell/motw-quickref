GREY VALLEY

It's 1985 and Eliza lives in a small town in a North American desert where she grew up before moving to the East Coast to attend college.

She owns and manages a hole in the wall coffee house that came with a cat that she hangs out with. Eliza has made many modifications to the building in her time there, including a secret mystical library and armory. Inside of the walls, the cofee house is also covered in secret protection glyphs that ward against many types of baddies.

BACKSTORY

Eliza, half Native American, was born in a desert somewhere in North Americass and was abandoned shortly thereafter. Her biological mother was white and her father was Navajo and Eliza was the result of a one night stand. She was discovered and adopted by a loving white family, and then brought up in nice home, but taught her parents' religion while being forbidden to seek more knowledge about her ancestors' religion. Her father was a strict religious leader in town.

She had a mostly uneventful childhood. She was taught to hunt and handle guns by her father. She did well in school and played basketball for the middle school and high school teams. She showed aptitude for math early on. It eventually led to her moving to the East coast attending the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Having never had alcohol or drugs, she was talked into taking magic mushrooms in her freshman year. During her first experience, Eliza believes that she was recruited and trained by a race of aliens tasked with protecting the earth from supernatural interdemensional monsters. She left the trip convinced that the aliens were gods from Native American lore that had chosen her because of her heritage and unique skills with computers.

Well on her way to becoming a professional programmer, she found the hallucinagenic experience overwhelming and dropped out of college soon after. She looked for any and all hallucinagens to try to talk to the aliens again, but she could never make contact with them again.

In the meantime, she took the task seriously and Eliza dedicated all of her time to studying the supernatural and the occult. She dropped out of college and moved back to the desert where she could feel more closely connected to her ancestral roots. She used her computer skills to create a database where she kept all of her findings -- the first of its kind in digital form.

Now an old woman, Eliza continues to study, hunt, and build upon her database. She is a legend in the crypto zoological scene, but most view her as a whackjob due to her alien stories and proclivity for being high on hallucinagens for days at a time. Regardless, she is the first call for many hunters in North America when they need advice.
